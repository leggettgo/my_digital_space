Why I Started This Site

Published on February 9, 2025

The comic series Asterix was a staple bedtime and bathroom read of my early childhood. I look back on the racist depictions with distaste (link to article on this), however the word play in the character names is a treasure of the stories that sticks with me still. Getafix the druid, Unhygienix the fishmonger, and Cacofonix the ill-talented bard all paled in comparison with my favourite animal companion Dogmatix, an orbital and often times pivotal character in each story. The English name is an even more fitting title than the original French name Idéfix, which plays on the French expression idée fixe (fixed idea).

Far from the quirky, loyal and stubborn character in those stories, to be dogmatic is a trait I find worrying and far too easily adopted. It underlies the principles of conservatism - keeping things the same because they have been kept the same - an ideology that stifles progress and upholds many violent social hierarchies.

As an individual level it is easy to be held back by your own deep rooted beliefs. A profound change I made at the round age of 20 was to follow a vegan lifestyle. The choice followed a slew of research into food systems, climate change, and animal ethics. Why, then, did I have such a strong opinion about the importance of consuming meat prior to this, when I did nothing to inform that behaviour? Looking back it is easy to see the impact of British cultural normalisation and a pressure to conform to masculine stereotypes; upbringing and environment were my biggest enablers.

Going vegan felt incredibly liberating. I had autonomy over a huge aspect of my lifestyle, and knew that my choices benefited other earthlings. However, I started to see things increasingly as black and white. I couldn't understand why everyone couldn't follow this path onto the other side of the curtain. Relationships were put under strain and social scenarios around food became awkward. The dogma had returned.

The years since then have given way to more important perspective shifts, and each learning experienced has emphasised the nuance of the previous. I am still an ethical vegan and hold many strong beliefs that shape my life, but I have slowly removed the sense of finality around them. For veganism this involves the understanding that morality is subjective (although I do think that it is largely ignorance that does not move the needle on this issue). It is important to me that the ideas I give weight to do not crush my critical consideration of new information. I want to learn and I want to change, because I know the world is not static.
