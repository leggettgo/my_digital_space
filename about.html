A Preface

It's crucial that you know 'who' I am to read the content on this site. I am primarily writing this as an exercise for myself, to put into words who I think I am. It also provides context for how I arrived at the creation of this site, so if that interests you please read on!

Me (Alex) (he/him)

I grew up in South London in the midst of a large and close family which provided familiar comfort and indeed my first ever friends. This experience of strong community would inspire me to look for similarly reliable networks throughout my life, and I still struggle to feel content with my own company in times when these are absent. My parents valued education and work, while I valued pleasing others, so I was suitably studious in school and performed well against the metrics of the British schooling system.

I don't feel defined by my career or my education, but I suppose I will say I studied Geology at a London university and went on to work for 5 years in the Catastrophe Modeling field of Insurance. Positive feedback of a secure job with a good salary (and a lack of critical thinking on my part) are the primary reasons for my extended corporate stay, despite the bureaucracy, dogmatic hierarchies, and questionable ethics around the business model. Still, I count myself lucky to have learnt what I do and do not value about the world of work in such a privileged way.

At a confluence of some incredible new friendships I began to see the world and my position in it through a different lens. My job became secondary to the time I put in developing strong relationships and rethinking the existing connections around me. I began to learn about the tight-knit web of injustices that keep our lauded civilised society afloat, and with this knowledge my desire for change outgrew my want for corporate stability. In 2024 I happily joined moved with my partner from London to Amsterdam where they were to start a masters; it was an incredible opportunity to physically and ideologically cut ties with my current life structure.

I am writing this section five months into living in the Netherlands. Unlearning the habits and perceptions I have developed from a young age is not easy, however I now welcome the opportunity to be shown a new critical view of something I assumed benign. I have realised there is no 'perfect' purpose, occupation, or belief, but with the intention to support and show compassion to others one can contribute to a kinder world.
